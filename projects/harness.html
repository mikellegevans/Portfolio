Dirt Bike DEV1 — Wire Harness Redesign

Electric Vehicle Team · RIT
Feb 2026 – Present

As part of the Electric Vehicle Team, I worked on an undocumented wire harness on an electric dirt bike, helping trace what it was actually doing and rebuild it into something simpler and more reliable.

The problem

The dirt bike's wire harness was undocumented and overcomplicated, with no clear record of what each line did. Before anything could be improved, the existing system had to be understood from the ground up.

What I did

Working with the team, I helped audit the harness and work through root-cause and failure analysis, mapping the system against what it actually needed to do. I worked on identifying and removing redundant and unused wiring, which cut the harness complexity by roughly 80%, and contributed to rebuilding it using Deutsch and Anderson connectors for reliable, serviceable connections.

How I validated it

I validated the rebuilt harness through continuity and multimeter testing against the system's reliability requirements, confirming it minimized voltage drops and improved overall electrical reliability before it went on the vehicle.
